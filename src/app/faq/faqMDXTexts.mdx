### General Information

<details>
  <summary>
    #### What is Open Fresno?
  </summary>

Open Fresno is an independent community of technologists, developers, designers,
and civic-minded individuals who collaborate to use technology and open data to
address civic challenges and improve public services in the Fresno area. Open
Fresno was formerly known as Code for Fresno and was a local chapter of the
nationwide Code for America Brigade.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### How can I get involved with Open Fresno?
  </summary>

Getting involved is easy! You can join their vibrant community by attending their
events, meetups, and workshops. Whether you're an experienced coder or someone
eager to learn, there's a place for you to contribute your skills and passion to
create positive change in the community. For detailed directions on how to get
started, please refer to our [Get Started](/get-started) page.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Do I need to be a programmer to volunteer with Open Fresno?
  </summary>

Not at all! Open Fresno welcomes individuals from diverse backgrounds and skill
levels. While programming skills are valuable, we also need designers, data analysts,
project managers, and anyone enthusiastic about civic engagement and community
improvement.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### What kind of projects does Open Fresno work on?
  </summary>

Open Fresno works on a variety of civic tech projects. These projects could
involve creating web applications, mobile apps, data visualizations, or other digital
tools that address local civic issues and improve government services. See our
[Projects](/projects) page for more information.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Is Open Fresno affiliated with the government or any political organization?
  </summary>

Open Fresno is a non-partisan and independent organization. We
collaborate with government agencies and nonprofits based on project
and community impact and not political affiliation.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### What is the time commitment for volunteering with Open Fresno?
  </summary>

The time commitment is flexible, and you can contribute as much time as you can
spare. Whether it's attending occasional events or actively participating in ongoing
projects, your involvement can be tailored to your schedule and interests. However,
we find that our most successful and engaged volunteers commit to coming regularly
as it can take time get up to speed and contribute.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Are the projects open source?
  </summary>

Yes, all of the projects developed by Open Fresno are open source. Open source
means that the code and resources are freely available to the public, allowing
others to use, modify, and contribute to the projects.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Can I propose my own project idea?
  </summary>

Absolutely! Open Fresno encourages members to propose project ideas aligned
with the organization's mission. If you have a project idea that addresses a civic
challenge, they welcome your input and collaboration. See the [Pitch](/pitch) page.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Do I need to live in Fresno to participate?
  </summary>

While Open Fresno primarily focuses on projects in the Fresno area, participation is not limited to local
residents. Anyone interested in contributing remotely or learning from the community is welcome to join.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Is OpenFresno only for professionals?
  </summary>

No, Open Fresno welcomes anyone with a passion for civic engagement, regardless of their professional background.
Students, hobbyists, and individuals from all walks of life can make a meaningful impact within the community.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### Are there any age restrictions to volunteer?
  </summary>

There are no age restrictions to volunteer with us. We welcome individuals of all ages who are passionate about civic
tech and community engagement. Whether you're a student, a professional, or a retiree, you are welcome to join our
community and make a meaningful impact

</details>

### New Volunteers

<details>
  <summary>
    #### How can I become a new volunteer for Open Fresno?
  </summary>

To become a new volunteer for Open Fresno, you can start by filling out the "New Member Form" on our website.
Once you submit the form, someone from the team will send you a Slack invite to your email. Slack is where the Open
Fresno community communicates and collaborates, and it's a great way to get involved in ongoing projects and stay
updated on events and opportunities. We look forward to welcoming you to our community of civic-minded technologists
and passionate individuals!
You can access the New Member Form [here](https://forms.gle/JA3jAV9wE1645i9LA).

</details>

<details>
  <summary>
    #### What can I expect as a new volunteer?
  </summary>

As a new volunteer, you can expect a warm welcome from our community of civic-minded technologists and passionate
individuals. You'll have the opportunity to attend events, meetups, and workshops to connect with other members and
learn about ongoing projects. You can actively contribute your skills to projects aligned with your interests.

</details>

### Communication

<details>
  <summary>
    #### How do volunteers communicate and collaborate with Open Fresno?
  </summary>

Volunteers at Open Fresno use Slack and Meetup for seamless communication and collaboration. Slack serves as a
centralized platform for discussions, sharing ideas, and coordinating projects, while Meetup provides a hub for event
announcements and community gatherings. These tools enable our community of technologists and civic-minded individuals
to work together effectively, ensuring a dynamic and engaging volunteering experience.
Check out our Meetup [here](https://www.meetup.com/openfresno/).

</details>

### Working on Projects

<details>
  <summary>
    #### How can I get involved and work on a project with Open Fresno?
  </summary>

To work on a project with Open Fresno, visit the project page where you'll find detailed information and
instructions on how to contribute. Each project page provides valuable insights into the project's objectives, current
status, and the skills needed. This way, you can identify projects that align with your interests and expertise,
making it easy to actively participate and make a meaningful impact.

</details>

### Pitch a Project

<details>
  <summary>
    #### How can I pitch a project idea to Open Fresno?
  </summary>

Pitching a project idea is a fantastic way to contribute to Open Fresno's community. You can find the "Pitch a
Project" page easily accessible from the Navbar under "Get Involved." This page provides a platform for you to present
your project idea, outline its goals, and describe how it addresses a civic challenge. By following the guidelines
provided, you can introduce your idea to the community and gather support from like-minded volunteers who are
passionate about creating positive change.

</details>
